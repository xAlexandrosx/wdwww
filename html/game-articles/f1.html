Developer: Codemasters
Publisher: EA Sports
Release Date: May 30, 2025 (Iconic Edition: May 27, 2025)
Platforms: PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, PC
Genre: Racing Simulation

Overview

F1® 25 is the latest installment in the official Formula 1 racing simulation series, developed by Codemasters and published by EA Sports. This edition marks a significant leap forward, offering enhanced realism, updated gameplay modes, and a deeper connection to the world of Formula 1.

Key Features

  • Braking Point 3: The narrative-driven story mode returns, focusing on the fictional Konnersport team as they vie for championship glory. Players' decisions influence the storyline, offering a cinematic racing experience.
  • My Team 2.0: The team management mode has been overhauled, allowing players to step into the role of a team owner. New features include the ability to manage driver line-ups and car development strategies more intricately.
  • Enhanced Realism: Utilizing LiDAR technology, tracks like Bahrain, Miami, Melbourne, Suzuka, and Imola have been laser-scanned for unparalleled accuracy. The game also introduces reverse layouts for circuits such as Silverstone, Zandvoort, and the Red Bull Ring, adding a fresh challenge.
  • F1® The Movie Integration: In collaboration with the upcoming Formula 1 film starring Brad Pitt, the game features the fictional APXGP team and scenarios inspired by the movie, blurring the lines between gaming and cinema.
  • Next-Gen Exclusivity: Designed exclusively for current-generation consoles and PC, F1® 25 leverages advanced hardware capabilities to deliver improved graphics, faster load times, and more responsive gameplay.

Gameplay Enhancements

The game introduces refined physics and handling models, offering a more authentic driving experience. AI opponents have been improved for more competitive and realistic races. Additionally, the inclusion of real team radio communications and revamped podium ceremonies enhances immersion.

Reception

F1® 25 has been met with positive reviews, with critics praising its commitment to realism, the depth of its management systems, and the engaging narrative of Braking Point 3. The integration of cinematic elements and the focus on next-gen platforms have been highlighted as standout features.

Conclusion

With its blend of authentic racing simulation, compelling storytelling, and innovative features, F1® 25 sets a new benchmark for Formula 1 games. Whether you're a seasoned fan or new to the series, this installment offers a comprehensive and immersive racing experience.
