Founded: February 8, 1991 (as Silicon & Synapse)
Founders: Michael Morhaime, Frank Pearce, Allen Adham
Headquarters: Irvine, California, USA
Industry: Video Game Development and Publishing

Overview

Blizzard Entertainment is one of the most iconic video game developers in the world, known for creating groundbreaking franchises that have shaped the gaming industry. Originally founded as Silicon & Synapse, the company became Blizzard Entertainment in 1994 and has since developed beloved titles spanning strategy, role-playing, and online multiplayer genres.

Iconic Franchises

Blizzard has created several legendary franchises, each with a lasting cultural impact:

  • Warcraft: Starting as a real-time strategy series in 1994, it evolved into the world-renowned MMORPG World of Warcraft.
  • StarCraft: A sci-fi real-time strategy game that became a competitive esports phenomenon, especially in South Korea.
  • Diablo: An action RPG series praised for its dark atmosphere, loot-based progression, and fast-paced combat.
  • Overwatch: A team-based hero shooter that redefined the FPS genre with vibrant characters and accessible gameplay.
  • Hearthstone: A digital card game based on Warcraft lore that brought collectible card games to a mainstream audience.

World of Warcraft and Online Gaming

Released in 2004, World of Warcraft (WoW) became a cultural milestone in online gaming. With millions of active subscribers at its peak, WoW pioneered many aspects of MMORPG design and remains active today through ongoing expansions and a dedicated fanbase.

Esports and Community

Blizzard has been a strong supporter of esports with games like StarCraft II, Overwatch, and Hearthstone. The Overwatch League was one of the first attempts to professionalize esports with city-based teams and structured seasons. Blizzard also nurtures a dedicated community through conventions like BlizzCon, a fan-focused event showcasing announcements, esports, and developer panels.

Challenges and Evolution

In recent years, Blizzard has faced criticism regarding corporate decisions, workplace culture, and game delays. Despite this, the studio continues to update and expand its franchises while pursuing new directions, such as Diablo IV and Overwatch 2.
