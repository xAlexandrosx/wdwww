Developer: EA Vancouver, EA Romania
Publisher: Electronic Arts (EA Sports)
Release Date: October 1, 2021
Platforms: PC, PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox One, Xbox Series X|S, Nintendo Switch, Stadia
Genre: Sports Simulation

Overview

FIFA 22 is a football simulation game developed by EA Sports and marks the 29th installment in the long-running FIFA series. It introduced groundbreaking features powered by next-gen technology, offering the most realistic football gameplay experience to date—especially on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S.

Key Features

  • HyperMotion Technology: Exclusive to next-gen consoles, this new motion capture system combines advanced machine learning with real-world match capture to enhance realism in player movement, animations, and behavior.
  • Career Mode Enhancements: Players could now create their own football clubs from scratch, customize kits and stadiums, and experience deeper management features.
  • FIFA Ultimate Team (FUT): FUT 22 introduced a redesigned Division Rivals and FUT Champions system, focusing on player progression and rewards.
  • Volta Football: The street-style football mode returned with new seasonal content, improved gameplay, and signature abilities that added arcade-like flair to the experience.
  • Improved Goalkeeper System: A complete rewrite of the goalkeeper mechanics brought smarter decision-making, better animations, and distinct styles for different keepers.

Licensing and Teams

FIFA 22 retained its massive library of over 700 teams, 17,000+ players, and more than 90 licensed stadiums. However, some clubs such as Juventus and Roma were still licensed under different names due to exclusivity agreements with competitors like Konami’s eFootball.

Reception and Impact

Upon release, FIFA 22 received praise for its gameplay improvements—especially on next-gen systems—and deeper Career Mode. Critics noted it was one of the most polished FIFA entries in years. It also maintained a massive global player base and strong microtransaction revenue through FUT.

Legacy

FIFA 22 was the penultimate game released under the "FIFA" brand before EA Sports parted ways with FIFA to launch EA Sports FC in 2023. As such, FIFA 22 marked a transitional period, combining innovation with the legacy that defined the series for over two decades.

Conclusion

FIFA 22 was a landmark in the franchise’s evolution—bridging the gap between console generations while pushing realism and customization to new heights. Whether managing a club, building a FUT squad, or hitting the streets in Volta, FIFA 22 delivered a deep and engaging football simulation experience for fans worldwide.
